I. Introduction & Objectives

This interactive guide covers the fundamental skills of network cabling. You will learn to construct and test the two most common types of Ethernet cables: straight-through and crossover. Mastering this skill is essential for any networking professional for setting up and troubleshooting wired networks.

Learning Objectives

  • ✓Identify UTP cable components and understand T568A/T568B wiring standards.
  • ✓Construct functional straight-through and crossover cables.
  • ✓Properly use essential network cabling tools like crimpers and testers.
  • ✓Test cables for continuity and correct pinout.

V. Interactive Procedures

This section provides a hands-on guide to creating both straight-through and crossover cables. Use the tabs below to switch between the procedures. Pay close attention to the interactive wiring diagrams to master the color codes.

Straight-Through Construction (T568B)

  1. Preparation: Carefully strip about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of the outer jacket from the cable end.
  2. Arrange Wires: Untwist and straighten the wires. Arrange them in the T568B sequence shown in the diagram.
  3. Trim & Insert: Pinch the wires, trim them to a uniform length (~0.5 inch), and slide them fully into an RJ-45 connector. Ensure the jacket is also inside the connector for strain relief.
  4. Crimp & Test: Place the connector in the crimping tool and squeeze firmly. Repeat for the other end using the same T568B standard. Test with a LAN tester for a 1-to-1 pinout.

Crossover Construction (T568A to T568B)

  1. Prepare First End (T568B): Follow the complete procedure for a straight-through cable to terminate one end using the T568B standard.
  2. Prepare Second End (T568A): On the other end, arrange the wires according to the T568A standard shown in its diagram.
  3. Crimp Second End: Trim, insert, and firmly crimp the T568A end.
  4. Test the Cable: Use a LAN tester. The lights will show a "crossed" pattern for pairs 2 and 3 (pins 1, 2, 3, 6).
