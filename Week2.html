Welcome to Week 2 of Networking 2! This week, we'll dive deeper into the foundational aspects of computer networks, exploring the telecommunication landscape in the Philippines and fundamental concepts that underpin all network communication.

Part 2: Core Network Fundamentals and Components

Building upon our brief introduction last week, we will now explore the fundamental concepts and essential components that make up computer networks.

1. Computer Networks Overview

We'll reiterate and expand on the definition of computer networks, emphasizing their purpose and the benefits they provide.

2. Types of Networks: LAN, MAN, WAN

Networks are categorized based on their geographical scope. We will examine the characteristics and common uses of:

  • LAN (Local Area Network): Networks covering a small, localized area like an office building or home.
  • MAN (Metropolitan Area Network): Networks spanning a city or a large campus.
  • WAN (Wide Area Network): Networks connecting geographically dispersed areas, often spanning countries or continents (e.g., the Internet).

3. Network Edge: Client and Server Programs

The "network edge" refers to the end systems (hosts) that connect to the network. We'll discuss the interaction between:

  • Clients: Devices (like your computer or smartphone) that request services.
  • Servers: Powerful computers that provide services (e.g., web servers, email servers) to clients.

Understanding this client-server model is fundamental to how applications function over a network.

4. Access Networks

Access networks are the physical connections that link end systems to the network edge. We'll cover various types, such as DSL, cable, fiber-to-the-home, and wireless access technologies.

5. Network Topology

Network topology refers to the arrangement of elements (links, nodes, etc.) in a communication network. We'll explore common topologies, including:

  • Bus Topology: A single cable connecting all devices.
  • Star Topology: All devices connected to a central hub or switch.
  • Ring Topology: Devices connected in a circular fashion.
  • Mesh Topology: Every device connected to every other device.
  • Hybrid Topologies: Combinations of the above.

Understanding topology helps in designing and troubleshooting networks efficiently.

6. Network Components

We'll identify and discuss the roles of essential hardware components in a network:

  • Routers: Forward data packets between computer networks.
  • Switches: Connect devices within a single LAN.
  • Hubs: Older devices that connect multiple Ethernet devices together.
  • Modems: Convert digital signals to analog for transmission over lines (and vice-versa).
  • Network Interface Cards (NICs): Hardware that allows a computer to connect to a network.
  • Cables: Physical media (e.g., Ethernet cables) used for connections.
